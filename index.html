Hitesh Ulhas Vaidya

I am a graduate student with major in Computer Science at the Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT). Currently, I am pursuing my graduate thesis on overcoming catastrophic forgetting in lifelong learning neural networks.

At RIT, I am advised by Dr. Alexander Ororbia II. I am also a member of NAC lab. I completed my Bachelor of Engineering (BE) from Vidyalankar Institute of Technology which is affiliated to the University of Mumbai.

I am interested in building an intelligent system that is evolving in nature and which reuses its current knowledge to learn new tasks. My current thesis work involves researching how we could use the ability of a human brain to selectively learn, remember and forget knowledge throughout its lifetime, in order to train a lifelong learning based neural network. The aim of this research is to alleviate catastrophic forgetting using such techniques.

Stream-51: Streaming Classification and Novelty Detection From Videos
Ryne Roady, Tyler Hayes, Hitesh Vaidya, Christopher Kanan
CVPR Workshop on Continual Learning, 2020
Video  /  code

In this work, we introduce Stream-51, a new dataset for streaming classification consisting of temporally correlated images from 51 distinct object categories and additional evaluation classes outside of the training distribution to test novelty recognition. We establish unique evaluation protocols, experimental metrics, and baselines for our dataset in the streaming paradigm.
